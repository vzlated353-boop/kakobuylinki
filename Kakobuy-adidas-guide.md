## Quality Verification and Material Guide for Adidas Products

Understanding the quality verification process and material options is essential when purchasing Kakobuy adidas products. Adidas uses a variety of materials across different product lines, each with specific quality indicators. This section explains how to navigate these choices and what to look for during quality checks.

The quality verification system on Kakobuy operates through a structured QC photo process. After you place an order, the platform's quality control team photographs the specific products you will receive, including multiple angles, close-ups of materials and branding, and detail shots of construction quality. These QC photos are uploaded to your order dashboard, allowing you to review the actual products before they ship. This transparency is one of the key reasons why 92% of buyers report satisfaction with their Adidas purchases.

When reviewing QC photos for Adidas products, focus on these critical quality indicators:

**Three-Stripe Branding Accuracy**: The Three-Stripe logo is Adidas's most recognizable design element, and its accuracy is crucial. On shoes, the stripes should be properly positioned on the lateral side with consistent width and spacing. The stripes should be either embroidered, printed, or made of leather/suede overlays, depending on the model. Check that the stripes match reference images in terms of size, placement, and color. On tracksuits and apparel, the stripes should run straight along the sleeves and legs without wavering. The stripes should have sharp edges without bleeding or fading. Compare the QC photos against reference images of retail Adidas products to assess the Three-Stripe accuracy.

**Material Quality and Texture**: Adidas uses various materials depending on the product line. For leather shoes like Superstars and Stan Smiths, high-quality batches use genuine leather with a consistent grain pattern and natural feel. The leather should have a slight give when pressed, indicating proper tanning and finishing. For suede shoes like Campuses, the suede should have a soft, velvety texture with even nap direction. For Primeknit uppers on Ultraboosts and NMDs, the knit should be tight and consistent with no loose threads or visible defects. For tracksuits, the polyester blend should feel smooth and comfortable with consistent color and no visible flaws. Lower-quality batches may use synthetic materials that feel plasticky or have inconsistent texture.

**Boost Midsole Quality**: On Ultraboost and NMD models, the Boost midsole is a key quality indicator. High-quality Boost midsoles feature the distinctive textured surface with thousands of tiny TPU pellets visible. The Boost should have a consistent white or off-white color with no yellowing or discoloration. The midsole should feel firm yet responsive when pressed. On NMD models, the Boost plugs on the lateral and medial sides should be properly positioned and securely attached. Lower-quality batches may have Boost midsoles that look too smooth, have inconsistent color, or feel too hard or too soft.

**Stitching and Construction**: Examine the stitching along the upper panels, toe box, heel counter, and outsole attachment points. Quality Adidas products feature even, tight stitching with no loose threads or uneven spacing. The stitch count per inch should be consistent, typically around 8 to 10 stitches per inch on premium batches. Pay attention to the connection points where different materials meet, as these areas often reveal quality differences. On tracksuits, check the stitching along the zipper, pockets, and waistband. The stitching should be straight and secure with reinforced stitching at stress points.

**Sizing Considerations**: Adidas sizing can vary between different product lines. Running shoes like Ultraboost typically fit true to size, while lifestyle shoes like Superstars may run slightly large. Campus shoes generally fit true to size with a comfortable, relaxed fit. For tracksuits, Adidas sizing tends to be athletic but not overly tight. For detailed sizing guidance across all products, refer to the [Kakobuy sizing guide](/Kakobuy-sizing-guide/) before placing your order. Community feedback indicates that Adidas products from higher-rated batches tend to have more consistent sizing compared to budget-tier options. Most buyers recommend checking batch-specific sizing notes in the spreadsheet, as fit can vary between manufacturers.

Batch version ratings on Kakobuy typically range from budget to premium. Premium batches offer the closest quality to retail standards, with accurate materials, proper Three-Stripe branding, and reliable construction. Budget batches are more affordable but may show visible differences in material quality and overall finishing. Most experienced buyers recommend investing in mid-tier or premium batches for Adidas products, as the material quality difference is particularly noticeable in terms of comfort and durability.
