## Tip 2: Master the QC Inspection Process

Quality control photos are not just a formality. They are your best opportunity to catch issues before a product crosses international borders. The platform maintains a 94% quality pass rate, but experienced buyers know that even within that 94%, there are degrees of quality. Here is the QC inspection checklist that top buyers follow:

First, examine stitching quality. Look for even, consistent stitches with no loose threads or irregular patterns. On items like the Palm Angels Hoodie at $28.85, pay special attention to the iconic sleeve logos, as misaligned patches are one of the most common QC issues. Second, check color accuracy against reference images. A Stone Island T-shirt at $14.01 should match the expected color tone; slight variations between batches are normal, but significant deviations warrant a replacement request.

Third, inspect logos and hardware. Zippers, buttons, and embossed logos should be clean and well-defined. On designer pieces like the Dior B22 at $45.33, logo placement is critical. Fourth, assess material texture and weight. The fabric should look substantial, not thin or translucent. Community guides on [Kakobuy qc photos](/Kakobuy-qc-photos/) provide detailed examples of what to look for across different product categories.

## Tip 3: Optimize Your Shipping Strategy

Shipping is where many buyers lose money or experience unnecessary delays. Standard shipping to the US and EU typically takes 10-14 days, but there are ways to optimize this process. First, consolidate orders when possible. Combining multiple items into a single shipment reduces per-item shipping costs significantly. A buyer ordering a Trapstar Hoodie at $26.04 and a pair of Nike Shorts at $14.18 saves roughly $5 to $8 by shipping them together rather than separately.

Second, choose your shipping method wisely. Express shipping costs more but includes better tracking and insurance, which is worth the premium for high-value items like the Moncler Down Jacket at $78.57. For lower-cost accessories like phone cases at $1.30 or socks at $6.59, standard shipping is perfectly adequate. Third, be aware of peak seasons. Ordering during Chinese holidays or major shopping events can add a week or more to processing times. Plan accordingly and order at least two weeks before you need an item.

## Tip 4: Build a Wishlist and Track Prices

Patient buyers save the most money. Instead of impulse purchasing, create a wishlist of items you want and monitor their prices over time. The spreadsheet makes this easy, as you can sort by category and track price changes. A buyer who wants a pair of Yeezy 350s at $32.97 might notice that the price drops to $29.50 during a less busy period, or that a new batch with better reviews becomes available.

Tracking also helps you identify which products have consistent quality across batches versus those that vary significantly. The Chrome Hearts Jeans at $26.04, for instance, tend to have stable quality because they come from a limited number of factories. In contrast, popular items like the Nike Dunk at $57.69 may have wider quality variation across batches. By tracking over time, you learn which products are safe to buy immediately and which require patience.
