Consider the Nike Air Max 95, priced at $46.15 with over 410 orders recorded. This model typically runs half a size smaller than standard US sizing. A buyer who normally wears US 10 and orders without checking may receive a shoe that feels tight across the toe box. The same applies to apparel. An Essentials Hoodie at $31.15 might fit differently depending on whether it comes from the mid-weight or heavy-weight batch. The [Kakobuy sizing guide](/Kakobuy-sizing-guide/) covers brand-specific recommendations, measurement charts, and batch variations that can save you from an ill-fitting purchase.

Experienced buyers always cross-reference their measurements against the size chart provided for each specific batch version. This takes less than two minutes but prevents weeks of delay if you need to request an exchange. ## Mistake 3: Underestimating Total Landed Cost

Many newcomers focus solely on the listed product price and forget to calculate the full landed cost. A Trapstar Hoodie listed at $26.04 might seem like a straightforward purchase, but shipping, potential customs duties, and payment processing fees add to the final amount. Buyers who budget only for the product price often experience sticker shock when the total arrives.

The platform typically charges shipping based on weight and destination. Standard shipping to the US or EU ranges from $8 to $15 for most clothing items and $12 to $25 for shoes and heavier items. Customs duties vary by country but typically apply to orders exceeding certain thresholds. Smart buyers calculate the total cost before committing and factor in a 15-20% buffer above the listed price.

New buyers who simply select the cheapest option without researching batch reviews frequently end up disappointed. Spending an extra ten minutes researching batches can mean the difference between a product you love and one you regret.
