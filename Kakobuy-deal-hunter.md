## Deal Verification and Quality Assessment

Not every low price represents a genuine deal. A $15 sneaker that falls apart after two weeks costs more than a $28 pair that lasts for years. Effective deal hunters evaluate value through the lens of quality-adjusted pricing rather than absolute cost alone.

Quality pass rates provide the most reliable indicator of actual value. Products achieving 92-95% quality pass rates consistently deliver on their promises. Items below 85% typically lead to returns, dissatisfaction, and wasted expenditure. Always check seller ratings (aim for 4.8+ stars) and recent reviews mentioning quality consistency.

Request comprehensive QC photos before confirming any deal. Focus on stitching quality, material weight, logo accuracy, and hardware functionality. Compare against authentic product references. The 10-15 minutes invested in verification prevents hours of disappointment and return processing.

Establish quality benchmarks by price tier. Budget sneakers at $15-25 should show even stitching and consistent materials. Items at $25-35 should demonstrate additional attention to detail. Understanding these expectations prevents overpaying for mediocre quality and helps you recognize when a higher-priced item actually represents better value. ## FAQ

**How do I spot genuine deals versus marketing tricks?**
Compare current prices against your tracked price history for that specific product and seller. A genuine deal represents a 15%+ drop from historical norms, not just a "was/now" comparison that may use inflated reference prices. Check seller ratings and quality pass rates—low prices from unreliable sellers aren't deals, they're risks.

**What's the best time of day to hunt for deals?**
Early morning (6-9 AM China Standard Time) often reveals new listings and price adjustments made overnight. End-of-month sees sellers adjusting prices to hit targets. Major sales events typically launch at midnight CST. Set your schedule to capture these windows.

**How quickly should I act when I find a great deal?**
Genuine deals from reliable sellers should be acted on within 24-48 hours. Flash sales may require same-day action. However, never skip QC verification—taking 10-15 minutes to verify quality prevents costly mistakes. If you're uncertain about a seller, proceed with caution even if the price is excellent.

**How do I track deals across multiple sellers and categories efficiently?**
Use a spreadsheet to track products, sellers, prices, and dates. Update it daily during your monitoring routine. Screenshot listings when you see good prices. Over time, this creates a comprehensive price history that makes genuine deals immediately obvious. Consider browser bookmarks organized by category for quick access.
