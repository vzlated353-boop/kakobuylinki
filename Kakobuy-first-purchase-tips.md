## Avoiding Common First-Time Buyer Mistakes

Many first-time buyers make avoidable mistakes that can lead to disappointment or additional costs. One common error is ordering the wrong size due to inadequate research. Always consult sizing guides and read buyer reviews before selecting sizes. If you're between sizes, consider sizing up, as it's easier to alter clothing that's slightly large than to deal with items that are too small.

Another frequent mistake is underestimating shipping costs and delivery times. First-time buyers often focus solely on product prices without factoring in shipping fees, which can add $8 to $25 or more to the total cost. Similarly, expecting delivery in just a few days leads to frustration when standard shipping takes 10 to 14 business days. Understanding the complete timeline and cost structure helps you set realistic expectations.

Failing to inspect items thoroughly upon arrival is another common oversight. The unboxing process is your opportunity to verify product quality and identify any issues while you still have recourse for exchanges or returns. Take time to examine stitching, materials, colors, and functionality. Document any concerns with clear photos and contact customer service promptly if problems are identified.

Ordering from unfamiliar sellers or choosing extremely low-priced options without researching quality can also lead to disappointment. Stick with products that have strong order histories and high quality pass rates. The platform's 72% repurchase rate indicates that most buyers have positive experiences and return for additional purchases, but starting with proven products reduces risk for first-time buyers.
