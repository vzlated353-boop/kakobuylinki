## Quality Standards for Die-Cast Vehicles

Quality is the most important factor when purchasing die-cast models online, and Kakobuy has developed a rigorous verification system to ensure buyers receive products that meet their expectations. The quality control process for Hot Wheels-compatible models involves several key checkpoints that evaluate both visual appeal and structural integrity.

The first checkpoint is **paint quality**. Inspectors examine each model for even coverage, color accuracy, and adhesion. Common issues like paint bleeding, uneven metallic flake distribution, or chips on edges are flagged during inspection. Models that pass this stage typically score above 90% on the paint quality index. The second checkpoint is **body proportion accuracy**. Using reference images from the original vehicle designs, quality inspectors compare the die-cast model's dimensions, panel lines, and surface details against known specifications. Sets that deviate significantly from reference proportions are sent back for correction.

The third checkpoint focuses on **wheel and chassis quality**. Wheels must spin freely without wobbling, and the axle must be securely attached. The chassis should sit level on a flat surface, and any removable parts (hoods, doors, trunks) must fit snugly without excessive gaps. Based on platform data, the overall quality pass rate for die-cast models is 92%, with paint-related issues accounting for the majority of failures.

Community feedback reinforces these quality standards. With over 50,000 active members, the Kakobuy community regularly shares unboxing photos, comparison shots, and detailed reviews. According to platform data, 70% of die-cast car buyers order at least two additional models within three months, reflecting strong satisfaction with the initial purchase.
