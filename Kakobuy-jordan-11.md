When reviewing QC photos for Jordan 11s, focus on these critical quality indicators:

**Patent Leather Quality**: The Jordan 11's signature feature is its patent leather mudguard. High-quality batches use patent leather with a deep, mirror-like shine that does not crease excessively. Lower-quality batches may use synthetic materials that appear dull or develop premature cracking. Compare the QC photos against reference images of retail Jordan 11s to assess the shine and texture.

**Stitching and Construction**: Examine the stitching along the patent leather edges, the mesh upper panels, and the heel counter. Quality Jordan 11s feature even, tight stitching with no loose threads or uneven spacing. The heel tab should display a cleanly embossed Jumpman logo with proper proportions. Pay attention to the connection points where the patent leather meets the mesh upper, as these areas often reveal quality differences.

**Outsole and Midsole Details**: The herringbone traction pattern should be sharply defined with clean edges. The carbon fiber spring plate on the midsole should display accurate weave patterning and proper positioning. Translucent outsoles (on Concord and Space Jam variants) should show consistent clarity without cloudiness or discoloration.

**Sizing Considerations**: Jordan 11s generally fit true to size for most foot shapes. However, the patent leather upper has minimal stretch during the break-in period, so buyers with wider feet may want to consider going half a size up. Community feedback indicates that Jordan 11s from higher-rated batches tend to have more consistent sizing compared to budget-tier options. Always check batch-specific sizing notes in the spreadsheet before ordering.
