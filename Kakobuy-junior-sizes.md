## Measurement Techniques for Junior Products

Accurate junior measurement requires proper technique and understanding of changing body proportions. Use a flexible measuring tape and measure over lightweight clothing. For teens, measure while standing naturally. Record all measurements in centimeters for consistency with seller charts.

Chest measurement requires wrapping the tape around the fullest part of the chest, keeping it level across the back. For boys, measure at the nipple line. For girls, measure at the bust line. Have the teen stand naturally with arms at sides. This measurement proves crucial for shirts, jackets, and dresses where chest fit affects comfort and appearance.

Waist measurement should be taken at the natural waistline or where pants typically sit. For boys, measure at the narrowest point above the hips. For girls, measure at the natural waist or where they prefer waistbands. Keep the tape level and snug but not tight.

Hip measurement requires wrapping the tape around the fullest part of the hips. For girls, this becomes increasingly important during puberty as hip proportions change.
