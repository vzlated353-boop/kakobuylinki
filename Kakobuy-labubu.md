## Quality Verification for Art Toys

Art toys demand a different quality standard than traditional collectibles, and Kakobuy has adapted its verification process to meet these specific requirements. For Labubu figures, the inspection process focuses on three critical areas: sculpt fidelity, paint application, and packaging condition.

**Sculpt fidelity** is the first checkpoint. Inspectors compare each figure against reference images from the original Pop Mart releases, checking that the proportions, facial features, and surface textures match the expected design. Common issues like mold lines, air bubbles in resin, or misaligned parts are flagged. Figures that pass this stage maintain the characteristic Labubu aesthetic — oversized heads, sharp teeth, and expressive eyes.

**Paint application** is the second checkpoint. Labubu figures are known for their bold color palettes and fine detail work. Inspectors examine paint coverage for consistency, checking for bleeding, overspray, or missed spots. Special attention is given to small details like eye pupils, teeth shading, and accessory coloring. The average paint quality pass rate for Labubu figures is 91%, with blind box figures showing slightly lower rates due to the high-speed production process.

**Packaging condition** is especially important for art toy collectors, many of whom value box condition as much as the figure itself. Kakobuy inspectors check each box for dents, creases, color fading, and seal integrity. Figures are photographed inside their packaging before shipment, giving buyers confidence in the condition they will receive.

Community feedback plays a vital role in maintaining these standards. The Kakobuy art toy community includes over 15,000 active members who share unboxing videos, rate figures, and discuss quality variations between batches. According to platform data, 68% of Labubu buyers purchase at least one additional figure within four months, indicating strong repeat engagement.
