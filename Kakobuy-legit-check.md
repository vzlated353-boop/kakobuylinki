## How to Perform a Legit Check

Performing a thorough legit check requires a systematic approach. Follow these steps each time you receive QC photos for an order on Kakobuy:

**Step 1: Gather Reference Materials**
Before opening your QC photos, collect 3-5 high-quality reference images of the genuine product. Use trusted sources such as official brand websites, established authentication databases, or community-verified comparison posts. Having accurate references is the foundation of every successful Kakobuy legit check.

**Step 2: Compare Overall Silhouette and Shape**
Open the QC photos side by side with your reference images. Start with the overall profile — the shape of the toe box, the curve of the heel, the proportions of the midsole. Major shape deviations are the easiest red flags to spot and often indicate a fundamentally flawed batch. If the silhouette looks noticeably different from the genuine product, flag it immediately.

**Step 3: Inspect Material and Texture**
Zoom in on material surfaces. For leather products, check whether the grain pattern looks natural and consistent. For fabric items, verify that the weave density and texture match your references. Pay special attention to color — even slight shade differences can indicate a wrong batch or inferior materials. Compare under neutral lighting assumptions; warehouse photos use standardized lighting, so color discrepancies in the images are usually real.

**Step 4: Examine Stitching and Construction**
Look closely at all visible stitching lines. Genuine products feature even, tight stitching with consistent thread tension. Count the number of stitches per inch in critical areas and compare with references. Loose threads, uneven spacing, or doubled stitching in areas where single stitching should exist are strong indicators of poor quality. This step alone accounts for roughly 35% of all failed quality checks across product categories.

**Step 5: Verify Branding and Labels**
Check every logo, tag, and label against your references. This includes the main brand logo, size tags, interior labels, heel tabs, tongue tags, and box labels. Font weight, letter spacing, and positioning must all match precisely. Even experienced sellers occasionally ship products with incorrect label variants — this is one of the most commonly missed details.

**Step 6: Check Packaging and Accessories**
Don't neglect the box, tissue paper, extra laces, insoles, and any included accessories. Packaging quality often correlates with product quality. A flimsy box or misspelled branding on the tissue paper suggests the entire order came from a lower-tier batch.

**Step 7: Make Your Decision**
If everything checks out, approve the shipment. If you spot issues, use Kakobuy's replacement request feature within the available window. For borderline cases, consult community forums or comparison threads — experienced reviewers can often confirm whether a minor deviation is acceptable or a dealbreaker.

## Common Authentication Markers

Knowing what specific markers to look for separates casual reviewers from experienced authenticators. Here are the most reliable indicators used in every Kakobuy legit check:

**Stitch Count and Pattern**
Genuine products follow exact stitch count specifications. For example, a legitimate Nike Air Force 1 has exactly 13 stitches per inch on the quarter panel. Deviations of even 1-2 stitches per inch indicate a different manufacturing standard.

**Font and Typography**
Brand logos and text labels use proprietary fonts with precise kerning. Counterfeit products often use visually similar but technically incorrect typefaces. Pay particular attention to the weight of strokes, the shape of serifs, and the spacing between characters.

**Material Weight and Feel**
While you cannot feel products through photos, experienced buyers can identify material quality through visual cues. Genuine leather shows natural pore patterns and slight imperfections. High-quality synthetic materials have consistent texture. Cheap alternatives look overly uniform or plasticky.

**Placement and Alignment**
Logos, patterns, and design elements must be positioned with exact symmetry and alignment. A logo that sits 2mm too high or a pattern that doesn't align at the seam are telltale signs of inferior production standards.

**Hardware and Zippers**
For bags and accessories, hardware quality is a major authentication marker. Zippers should move smoothly, engravings should be crisp and centered, and metal finishes should be even. Community data indicates that hardware issues account for approximately 22% of all authentication failures in accessory categories.
