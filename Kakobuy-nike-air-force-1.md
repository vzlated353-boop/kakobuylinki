## Sizing and Fit Guide for Air Force 1

Proper sizing is crucial for ensuring comfort and satisfaction with your Air Force 1 purchase. The good news is that the AF1 Low '07 generally runs true to size for most buyers, making the ordering process straightforward. However, individual foot shape and personal preference can influence the ideal size, so understanding the nuances helps avoid fit issues.

For buyers with standard width feet, ordering your usual US size typically works well with the Air Force 1. The shoe features a padded collar and tongue that provide a snug but not restrictive fit around the ankle and instep. The toe box offers adequate room for toe splay, and the midfoot area provides secure lockdown without feeling overly tight. Most buyers report that their regular sneaker size translates directly to the AF1 without needing to adjust up or down.

Buyers with wider feet may benefit from going up half a size. The leather upper, while durable, has limited stretch initially, and a tighter fit can cause discomfort during the break-in period. Sizing up half a size provides additional room in the forefoot area without compromising heel lockdown. Conversely, buyers with narrow feet who find the standard size slightly roomy can try going down half a size, though this is less common.
