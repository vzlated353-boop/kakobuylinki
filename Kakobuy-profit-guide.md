## True Profit Calculation Methodology

Understanding true profit requires looking beyond simple price comparisons to comprehensive cost-benefit analysis that accounts for all factors affecting long-term value. This methodology applies whether you're evaluating personal purchases or business inventory decisions.

Total cost of ownership includes the purchase price plus all associated expenses: shipping costs, payment processing fees, return shipping if needed, and time investment for research and quality verification. A $25 item with $8 shipping and $1.50 in fees costs $34.50 total. Understanding true costs prevents underestimating expenses and overestimating profits.

Quality-adjusted cost per use provides the most accurate profit metric for personal purchases. A $30 hoodie that lasts 100 wears costs $0.30 per use, while an $18 hoodie that fails after 20 wears costs $0.90 per useˇŞmaking the more expensive item three times more profitable despite its higher upfront cost. This calculation reveals true value that simple price comparison misses entirely.

For resale purposes, net profit margin calculation must include all costs: product cost, shipping, payment processing (typically 3-5%), packaging materials, customer service time, and return handling. A sneaker sourced at $25 with $8 shipping, $1.50 fees, and $2 packaging costs totals $36.50. Selling at $70 yields $33.50 gross profit, but after accounting for 5% return rate and customer service time, net profit might be $28-30, representing 40-43% net margin.

Opportunity cost factors into profit calculations as well. Time spent researching, ordering, and handling issues has value. Efficient shoppers who develop systematic processes generate higher profit per hour invested than those who spend excessive time on each transaction. Optimizing for profit per hour rather than just profit per transaction leads to better overall returns.

For resale, subtract all costs from selling price to get net profit, then divide by selling price to get profit margin percentage. Track these metrics systematically to optimize returns.
