## Understanding QC Photos on Kakobuy

The QC photo system on Kakobuy operates as a structured quality verification workflow that sits between order fulfillment and shipment authorization. When you place an order through the platform, the seller sources the product and sends it to a Kakobuy-affiliated warehouse. These photographs ??the Kakobuy qc photos ??are then uploaded to your order dashboard, where you can review them and either approve the item for shipment or flag concerns for further inspection.

The standardized QC photo protocol typically includes between 6 and 12 images per item, depending on the product category. For footwear, you can expect shots of both shoes from multiple angles ??top view, side profile, heel, toe box, sole tread pattern, and interior labeling. For apparel items like hoodies and t-shirts, the photo set usually includes front and back views, close-ups of logo application or print quality, collar and cuff detail, interior care labels, and fabric texture shots. Bags and accessories receive similar treatment, with additional emphasis on hardware quality, zipper function, and interior construction.

Close-up detail shots reveal stitching quality, material texture, and print or embroidery clarity. Label and tag photos verify sizing information, brand markings, and care instructions. Interior shots expose construction methods that are not visible from the outside, such as lining quality, seam finishing, and internal pocket placement. Understanding what each photo is designed to communicate helps you read the QC set more efficiently and spot potential issues faster.

## How to Read QC Photos

Reading QC photos effectively requires a systematic approach rather than a casual glance. The difference between approving a product that meets your expectations and discovering problems after delivery often comes down to how carefully you examine each image. Follow this step-by-step process to develop a reliable QC photo review routine.

**Step 1: Start with the wide-angle overview shots**

Begin your review with the full-product photos that show the item from a distance. These images establish the baseline for color accuracy, overall proportions, and silhouette. Compare the wide-angle shots against the product listing images. Does the color match what was shown? Is the overall shape and proportion consistent with the listing? For sneakers, check that the sole profile and toe box shape align with reference images. For apparel, verify that the garment's drape and fit characteristics look correct. Discrepancies at this stage ??particularly in color ??are the most commonly reported issues and should be flagged immediately.

**Step 2: Examine close-up detail shots**

Move to the detailed close-up photos next. These images reveal the quality of stitching, print application, embroidery, and hardware. Look for even, consistent stitch lines with no loose threads or skipped stitches. For printed logos, check that the edges are clean, the color is saturated, and there is no cracking or bubbling in the print surface. For embroidered elements, verify that the stitching density is uniform and the design matches the listing exactly. On bags and footwear, inspect zippers, buckles, and other hardware for proper alignment and finish quality.

**Step 3: Check labels, tags, and interior construction**

Label photos verify that sizing information, brand markings, and material composition details are present and legible. Compare the size indicated on the label against what you ordered. Interior construction shots ??particularly important for bags, footwear, and lined garments ??reveal the quality of finishing that is not visible externally. Look for clean seam finishing, properly attached linings, and consistent material quality throughout.

**Step 4: Assess material texture and surface quality**

Fabric texture close-ups help you evaluate material weight, weave quality, and surface finish. For cotton garments, look for a tight, even weave with no visible thinning or inconsistency. For synthetic materials, check for proper texture replication ??the material should not appear overly shiny or plasticky. On leather or leather-alternative products, examine the surface grain pattern for consistency and natural appearance. If you are familiar with the retail version of the product, compare the material appearance against your knowledge of the authentic construction.

**Step 5: Make your decision**

After reviewing all photos systematically, assess whether the item meets your expectations. If everything looks correct, approve the item for shipment. If you identify concerns ??color mismatch, visible defects, incorrect sizing labels, or poor construction quality ??flag the specific issues with reference to the relevant photos. The Kakobuy quality assurance team will review your concerns and can request additional photos or initiate a return if warranted. ## Common Quality Issues to Watch For

Knowing what to look for in QC photos significantly improves your ability to catch problems before they become expensive mistakes. Based on platform inspection data and buyer feedback from 2026, the following quality issues appear most frequently across product categories.

**Color deviation** is the single most common issue, affecting approximately 8% of all orders. Products may arrive in a noticeably different shade than shown in listing images ??sometimes lighter, sometimes darker, and occasionally in an entirely different hue. This is particularly prevalent in apparel, where fabric dye lots can vary between production batches. Always compare QC photo colors against multiple listing images, not just one reference shot.

**Stitching defects** account for roughly 5% of flagged QC photos. Look for uneven stitch lengths, loose threads, skipped stitches, and seam puckering. On stress points like shoulder seams, armholes, and pocket openings, poor stitching is a durability concern that can lead to garment failure within weeks of regular wear.

**Print and logo quality issues** appear in approximately 6% of orders, particularly in graphic tees and branded hoodies. Common problems include misaligned prints, faded or oversaturated colors, cracking in puff-print applications, and logos that are shifted off-center. These issues are often subtle in listing photos but become apparent in the controlled lighting of QC photography.

**Material quality discrepancies** affect about 4% of orders. The fabric may appear thinner, shinier, or texturally different from what the listing suggests. On footwear, the sole material may look less dense or the wrong shade. On bags, the exterior material may lack the expected structure or weight.

**Sizing label errors** occur in approximately 3% of cases, where the physical label does not match the size ordered. This can result from warehouse picking errors or incorrect labeling at the manufacturing stage. Always verify that the size shown on interior labels matches your order specifications.

**Hardware and component issues** ??including misaligned zippers, poorly finished buckles, and weak magnetic closures ??appear in roughly 4% of orders for products that include these elements. These are best identified in close-up QC photos where hardware detail is clearly visible.
