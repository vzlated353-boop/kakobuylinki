## Sizing Considerations and Fit Recommendations

Choosing the right size is critical for sneaker satisfaction, and sizing can vary significantly across different brands and models. This section provides guidance on selecting the appropriate size for popular sneaker categories.

**Nike Sizing**: Nike shoes typically run true to size, making size selection straightforward for most buyers. If you normally wear a US 10 in Nike, order a US 10. However, some models may have slight variations. The Air Max 95 and Air Force 1 generally fit true to size, while some Dunk variants may run slightly narrow. If you have wide feet or prefer a more comfortable fit, consider going half a size up. The spreadsheet includes size availability for each model and variant.

**Jordan Sizing**: Jordan 4 typically fits true to size, but some buyers prefer going half a size up for additional comfort, especially if planning to wear thicker socks. The mesh panels and heel tab detailing are accurate across recent batches, with 320+ orders and 92% quality pass rate demonstrating consistent sizing and construction.

**Designer Brand Sizing**: Designer brands often have different sizing standards compared to Nike and Jordan. Check community feedback in the reviews section for model-specific sizing tips. Reading through recent buyer reviews can also reveal which batches run true to size and which require adjustment.

**Yeezy and Adidas Sizing**: Yeezy 350 typically runs small, and most buyers recommend going at least half a size up from your normal Nike size. Adidas shoes generally fit true to size, but some models may run slightly large or small depending on the design. The Yeezy 350 at ~$32.97 with 190+ orders and 93% quality pass rate is popular, but sizing accuracy is crucial for comfort.

**New Balance and Other Brands**: New Balance 9060 (~$42.86) typically fits true to size, offering comfortable cushioning for daily wear. Timberland Boots (~$65.93) may run slightly large, so consider going half a size down if you're between sizes. Always check the spreadsheet for size availability and community feedback for model-specific recommendations.

**Measuring Your Feet**: If you're unsure about your size, measure your feet at the end of the day when they're slightly swollen. Stand on a piece of paper, mark the longest point of your foot, and measure the distance from heel to toe. Compare this measurement to the brand's size chart. For Nike, a foot length of 10.6 inches typically corresponds to US 10. Different brands may have slightly different size charts, so always verify before ordering.
