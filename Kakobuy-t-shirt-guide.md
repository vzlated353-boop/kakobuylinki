## Sizing and Fit: Getting It Right the First Time

One of the most common mistakes buyers make when ordering t-shirts online is getting the size wrong. The Kakobuy t-shirt guide would not be complete without a detailed look at sizing and fit, since this is the area where most issues arise.

T-shirts on Kakobuy generally follow Asian sizing conventions, which tend to run slightly smaller than US or EU sizes. For most brands, going one size up from your usual fit will give you a comfortable, standard look. However, sizing can vary between brands and even between batches of the same brand. The Essentials T-shirt, for example, tends to fit closer to standard US sizing, while some Palm Angels and Amiri batches run noticeably smaller.

The spreadsheet includes size chart data for many of the t-shirt listings, with measurements for chest width, body length, and sleeve length. Compare these measurements against a t-shirt you already own that fits well. This is the most reliable way to choose the right size, as it removes the guesswork from brand-specific sizing quirks.

Community feedback also provides useful sizing insights. Buyers often share their experiences in reviews, noting whether a particular brand or batch runs small, true to size, or large. Pay attention to these data points, especially for brands you are ordering for the first time. If you are between sizes, most buyers recommend sizing up for t-shirts, since a slightly larger fit is more comfortable and versatile.
