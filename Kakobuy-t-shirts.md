## Fabric & Print Quality Guide

Understanding fabric composition and print quality is essential for evaluating whether Kakobuy t-shirts meet your expectations. The difference between a satisfying purchase and a disappointing one often comes down to details that are not immediately visible in product photos.

**Cotton Types and Weights**

The most common cotton types used in streetwear tees on Kakobuy are combed cotton, ring-spun cotton, and carded open-end cotton. Combed cotton, used in the Stussy and Palm Angels tees referenced above, undergoes an additional processing step that removes short fibers and impurities, resulting in a smoother, stronger yarn. Ring-spun cotton, found in the Trapstar and Essentials options, is spun to create a softer, more uniform thread that resists pilling better than carded varieties. Carded open-end cotton is the most economical option and is typically found in lower-priced blanks, though it can still produce acceptable results when the GSM weight is sufficient.

GSM (grams per square meter) is the standard measurement for fabric weight. In the streetwear tee category, 180 to 200 GSM is considered lightweight, 210 to 230 GSM is midweight, and 240 GSM and above is heavyweight. The products reviewed in this guide range from 210 GSM (Essentials) to 240 GSM (Palm Angels), with most buyers in 2026 showing a preference for the 220 to 240 GSM range, which provides substance and drape without excessive heaviness.

**Print Methods**

Screen printing remains the dominant decoration method for streetwear tees due to its durability and color vibrancy. Discharge printing, used on the Stussy tee, removes the garment's dye in the printed area and replaces it with the ink color, creating a soft, breathable print that becomes part of the fabric. Plastisol printing, common on the Trapstar tee, sits on top of the fabric and produces bold, opaque graphics, though it can feel stiff on larger print areas. Puff printing, used on the Essentials tee, adds a raised texture to the design and is a signature technique for the Fear of God sub-label.

For buyers evaluating print quality upon receipt, key indicators include edge sharpness (no bleeding or feathering), color consistency across units, and adhesion (the print should not crack or peel after the first wash when executed properly).

**Stitching and Construction**

Quality stitching is what separates a tee that lasts from one that falls apart after a few wears. Look for double-needle stitching on hems and sleeves, reinforced shoulder seams (often with twill tape), and clean finishing on the inside seams. The Palm Angels and Stone Island tees both feature reinforced construction that contributes to their higher price points and better longevity. Side-seam construction is standard across all five products reviewed, which provides better fit retention than tubular knit alternatives.

Collar quality is another critical factor. Ribbed collars with a higher cotton-to-elastane ratio (typically 95/5 or 93/7) maintain their shape through repeated wear and washing. Buyers should check that the collar lies flat without curling and that the seam joining it to the body is even and secure.
