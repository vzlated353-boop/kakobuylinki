## FAQ

**How do I calculate whether an item represents good value?**
Evaluate cost-per-use by considering predicted longevity relative to price. A $30 item that lasts 100 wears ($0.30/use) provides better value than an $18 item that lasts 20 wears ($0.90/use). Check material specifications (GSM weight for fabrics), quality pass rates (92%+), and seller ratings (4.8+ stars) to predict longevity accurately.

**What quality indicators should I prioritize when evaluating products?**
Focus on three critical areas: stitching consistency (even, tight stitches with no loose threads), material weight (substantial fabric that isn't transparent), and construction quality at stress points (reinforced seams, quality hardware). These indicators predict long-term durability more reliably than price alone.

**How can I build a versatile wardrobe on a budget?**
Start with neutral-colored basics that pair well together—white, black, gray, navy t-shirts and hoodies. Purchase core pieces in bundles from the same seller to ensure consistent sizing and unlock quantity discounts. Prioritize classic silhouettes over trendy pieces for maximum longevity and versatility.

**What's the best strategy for maximizing value across multiple purchases?**
Consolidate orders from the same seller to reduce per-item shipping costs by 40-60%. Build relationships with 3-5 reliable sellers across your preferred categories. Track your purchases and their longevity to refine your value predictions over time. Focus on quality pass rates of 92%+ rather than absolute lowest prices.
